PRIVACY POLICY

WEEKA APPLICATION

Effective Date: January 2025
Last Updated: January 2025

Article I: Fundamental Privacy Architecture

Section 1.1 - Zero-Collection Principle

Weeka operates under an absolute zero-collection architecture. No personal data, usage information, or device identifiers are collected, processed, transmitted, or stored by Weeka or any affiliated parties at any time, for any purpose.

Section 1.2 - Local-First Design Guarantee

All computational processes, data storage, and application functions execute exclusively on the user's device. No network communications occur except those explicitly initiated by the user through device-native sharing mechanisms.

Article II: Scope of Non-Collection

Section 2.1 - Categories of Data NOT Collected

Weeka affirmatively does not collect, access, or process:

  • Personal Identifiers Names, email addresses, phone numbers, account credentials, or any form of user identification
  • Device Information Device IDs, advertising identifiers, IP addresses, MAC addresses, or hardware specifications
  • Behavioral Data Usage patterns, interaction logs, performance metrics, or analytical data
  • Location Data GPS coordinates, network-based location, or any geographic information
  • Biometric Data Fingerprints, facial recognition data, or any biometric identifiers
  • Communications Messages, contacts, or any interpersonal data
  • Metadata Timestamps, session data, or operational metrics

Section 2.2 - Technical Implementation

The application binary contains no code for:

  • Automatic network transmission of user data
  • Remote telemetry collection
  • Third-party SDK integration for analytics
  • Server-side data endpoints
  • Cloud synchronization protocols
  • Account creation or authentication systems

Section 2.3 - Exception: Technical Support Logs

Weeka maintains technical debug logs stored exclusively on your device containing:

  • Application performance data
  • Error messages and warnings
  • Technical operations (no personal content)

These logs:

  • Remain on your device unless you explicitly choose to share them
  • Can be voluntarily sent to our support team if you request assistance
  • Can be deleted at any time through app settings
  • Contain no personally identifiable information by design

Article III: Data Sovereignty and User Rights

Section 3.1 - Absolute Data Ownership

Users maintain 100% ownership and control of all data created, modified, or stored through Weeka. This ownership is technically enforced through local-only storage architecture, not merely through policy.

Section 3.2 - User Rights (Self-Executing)

The following rights are automatically guaranteed through technical design:

  • Right to Access Direct device access to all data
  • Right to Deletion Complete removal via app uninstallation
  • Right to Portability Native export functionality including PDF generation
  • Right to Modification Unrestricted local editing
  • Right to Restriction No processing occurs beyond user device
  • Right to Control Debug logs remain local unless explicitly shared by user choice

Section 3.3 - Data Persistence and Deletion

  • Installation: Creates local storage container on device
  • Operation: All data remains in local container
  • Uninstallation: Complete and irreversible deletion of all associated data

Article IV: Security Framework

Section 4.1 - Local Security Measures

Data security is maintained through:

  • Application sandboxing per platform specifications
  • Standard file system permissions
  • No external attack surface (no server infrastructure)
  • Logs stored in application's private storage directory

Section 4.2 - Security Responsibilities

User Responsibilities:

  • Device-level security (passwords, biometric locks)
  • Physical device security
  • Backup management (if desired)

Developer Responsibilities:

  • Secure coding practices
  • Regular security updates
  • Prompt vulnerability patching

Article V: Legal Compliance

Section 5.1 - Regulatory Alignment

Weeka's zero-collection architecture inherently satisfies:

  • GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) No personal data processing occurs
  • CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act) No personal information sale or disclosure
  • COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) No collection from any users
  • PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act) No personal information handling
  • International Privacy Laws Universal compliance through non-collection

Section 5.2 - Legal Basis

No legal basis for processing is required as no processing occurs.

Article VI: Third-Party Interactions

Section 6.1 - No Third-Party Data Sharing

Weeka has no automatic capability to share data with third parties. No APIs, webhooks, or automatic data transmission protocols exist within the application.

Section 6.2 - User-Initiated Sharing

Users may choose to:

  • Export data to PDF format for local use or sharing
  • Send debug logs to our support team when requesting assistance
  • Use device-native sharing features to export data

Such user-initiated actions fall under user control and, once shared externally, are subject to the receiving party's privacy practices.

Section 6.3 - Support Communications

If you contact our support team and voluntarily share debug logs:

  • We use these logs solely to resolve your technical issue
  • Logs are deleted after issue resolution or within 30 days, whichever is sooner
  • No personal information should be contained in logs by design
  • You may request immediate deletion of shared logs at any time

Article VII: Special Provisions

Section 7.1 - Minors

As no data collection occurs, no age verification or parental consent mechanisms are required or implemented.

Section 7.2 - Sensitive Data Categories

No special categories of personal data (health, biometric, racial/ethnic origin, etc.) are collected or processed.

Section 7.3 - Cross-Border Transfers

No international data transfers occur as no data leaves the user's device.

Article VIII: Transparency Measures

Section 8.1 - Verifiable Privacy

Source code available at our GitHub repository for independent verification.

Section 8.2 - No Hidden Processes

  • No background processes collecting data
  • No dormant collection capabilities
  • No conditional collection based on user actions

Article IX: Policy Modifications

Section 9.1 - Amendment Procedure

Updates to this policy will be reflected in the "Last Updated" date. Substantial changes to the zero-collection principle would require a new application version and explicit user acceptance through app store update mechanisms.

Section 9.2 - Notification Limitations

As we possess no user contact information, policy updates are communicated through app store update notes and this document.

Article X: Dispute Resolution and Governing Law

Section 10.1 - Governing Law

As Weeka collects no personal data and operates entirely on user devices, privacy disputes are technically prevented. For any policy interpretations, this agreement shall be governed by the user's local jurisdiction, providing maximum legal protection under their own country's privacy laws.

Section 10.2 - Dispute Resolution

Given our zero-collection architecture:

  • No privacy violations can occur as no data exists to be violated
  • Any concerns are resolved through direct communication via email
  • Users retain all rights under their local consumer protection laws
  • Disputes, if any, shall be resolved through good faith dialogue

Article XII: Severability

If any provision of this Privacy Policy is held to be unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

Article XIII: Entire Agreement

This Privacy Policy constitutes the entire agreement between users and Weeka regarding privacy practices and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous understandings.

PRIVACY CERTIFICATION STATEMENT

Weeka implements Privacy by Design principles at the architectural level. Our zero-collection model represents the gold standard in privacy protection—not through promises or policies, but through technical impossibility of privacy violation.
